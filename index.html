Customer Insights

A comprehensive Power BI report on the effectiveness of a marketing campaign

View
Description:

Business question: Does email marketing meaningfully drive sales?

Solution: Built an interactive Power BI dashboard combining 12 months of sales and email engagement data, with dynamic segmentation and dedicated views for KPI trends, engagement funnel benchmarking, and regression analysis.

Key insights:

  • The campaign delivered strong engagement, confirming high reach and customer interaction.
  • Sales did not increase with engagement. Regression and statistical testing found no significant effect across segments.

Impact: Demonstrated a clear gap between engagement and revenue, preventing misattribution in performance reporting and shifting email strategy toward activation and retention.

Tools: SQL, Power BI, R (statistical validation)

Note: This dashboard version uses a reduced, anonymized subset of the original client's data.

Demo-Stat

A platform to navigate, visualize, and download Italian demographic indicators at the municipal, regional, and national level.

Online App
Description:

The Italian National Insitute of Statistics publishes demographic indicators at the national and regional level every year. However, it releases only raw demographic data for municipalities. Demo-Stat, an app developed in R and R Shiny, fills this gap by providing an interactive platform for exploring, downloading, visualizing, and interacting with demographic indicators for more than 7900 municipalities over the span of 20 years.

The application requests raw data from the National Institute of Statistics using istatAPI once a year. Then, the data is re-elaborated into five essential demographic indicators typically used in Research, which are then shown to the user in two distinct ways. The “Dashboard” view allows users to select a single municipality and observe all relevant indicators over a timespan of their choice. The “Plot & Compare” view enables users to compare how multiple municipalities perform on a single metric.

Waze Case Study

Using Statistical and Machine Learning models to predict user churn

Case Study
Description:

Waze is a community-driven GPS and navigational app that harness the power of its user base to provide real-time navigation information for route planning and optimization. This innovative approach leverages user-supplied data to deliver up-to-the-minute directions and traffic updates. However, the reliance on user data means that user churn, which refers to the number of users who stop using or uninstall the app, poses a significant challenge. High churn rates not only affect Waze’s revenue but also decrease the quality of service, as fewer users equate to less real-time traffic data.

The objective of this case study is to analyze user churn and understand the factors that drive this phenomenon. To this end, I developed a Binomial Regression Model and two Machine Learning Models: Random Forest and XGBoost. The insights gathered from this analysis can help Waze leadership optimize retention strategies, enhance user experience, and develop targeted retention strategies that address specific user needs and preferences.

Predicting Employee Turnover - HR Data

Analyzing HR data and developing statistical and machine learning models to predict employee turnover

Analysis
Description:

The dataset in this study contains several self-reported information about employees who worked in a company, such as satisfaction level, average monthly hours, and salary. This study aims to use machine learning and statistical techniques to develop models able to predict employee turnovers, understand what drives former employees to leave, and generate data-driven recommendations to increase retention.

I have developed a stable Random Forest model capable of predicting whether an employee is at risk of leaving with a confidence of above 90% and identifying the drivers of employee turnover. The analysis reveals that the employees represented in the data are generally overworked: most of them are assigned to several projects and work, on average, more than 200 hours a month. This effort, in turn, is not rewarded with promotions but with low evaluation scores and ultimately results in low satisfaction levels.
